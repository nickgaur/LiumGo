What is Hackathon ?

KEY POINTS TO KNOW ABOUT HACKATHON

You have already heard the term hackathon if you have some basic programming knowledge if not, then you are at the right place here you will know all about the hackathon.

  • What is a Hackathon?
  • What does everyone do at Hackathon?
  • What are the benefits if you are a part of a Hackathon?
  • How to participate in Hackathon?
  • How to find a Hackathon?
 What Is Hackathon?

Hackathon is an event organized by various tech companies, sometimes people have a wrong perception that it's an event that is organized by just only one organization! But it's a wrong perception.

Hackathon is an event where programmers get together for a short period of time to collaborate on a project. The participants work rapidly and often work without sleep to achieve their task, as the events generally only last 24 hours or take place over a weekend.

It is a competitive style event in which the project is to be done in a very short period of time. Many peoples attend or participate in dozens of hackathons but can win only a few it's quite tough to crack. In the hackathon participants have to build some prototypes like web applications, mobile apps, or other software.

2. What Do Everyone Do In Hackathon?

“Computer programmers and software designers collaborate and create a solution to an existing problem using technology,” Not only do programmers get the satisfaction of creating a useful product during the hackathon, but once the time runs out all participants come together and present their creations to a panel of judges Often large companies such as Google, Apple, Microsoft or Bloomberg will sponsor the event and give away some great prizes.

Speaking of prizes, it’s time to talk about the best part of hackathons: the benefits.

3. Benifits Of Participating In a Hackathon?

There are several benefits after participating in a Hackathon, But the best things are that one who participates gains a great experience to build good projects and offers a unique opportunity to build a powerful network. The best part of being in a hackathon is that you meet up with the people you interact with the people who care for your vision project to which you care the most. You meet up with some great persons who are masters of their fields you learn from them have the experience to work with such great people you get the things organized and also the speed you gain is remarkable.

Gurses adds that participating in (or winning) a hackathon is also a great resume-builder, especially for those with limited experience in the tech field. Even if participants don’t win the competition, the amount of knowledge a participant gains in such a short period of time is unmatched.

Another tangible perk of participating in a hackathon is the opportunity to win cash and other prizes. It’s unlikely for participants to go home empty-handed, according to Gurses. He says most hackathons give away things like t-shirts, mugs, bags, and even tech gadgets.

4. How To Participate In Hackathon?

This is an important question, especially for beginners looking to increase their involvement in the programming field. So whether you’re just dipping your toes into coding, you’re on your way toward earning a programming degree or you’re a seasoned programming pro – there’s a hackathon out there for you!

Most hackathons try to be as inclusive as possible, welcoming programmers of all experience levels. While there is a competitive aspect, many participants are just there to have fun, socialize and learn. If you’re unable to contribute anything to a team, you can always spectate. But our experts advise at least having some basic programming understanding if you are hoping to contribute and follow along.

5. How To Find a Hackathon?

Hackathons are happening all of the time all over the world. One of the best ways to find one near you just follow these websites they will surely inform you.

Here are a few to keep tabs on:

Hackathon.io

Major League Hacking

Hacker League

Devpost

One more way to find a hackathon is just having a google search and you will get any hackathon near your university or your tech center, there are also virtual hackathons in which you can participate from anywhere!
